Safety Tips for Swedish Wilderness

Sweden's wilderness is generally safe, but proper preparation and knowledge of local conditions are essential for a successful hiking experience.

Weather Awareness

Swedish weather can change rapidly, especially in the mountains:

  • Check SMHI (Swedish weather service) forecasts before departure
  • Be prepared for sudden temperature drops, even in summer
  • Carry rain gear year-round
  • Respect weather warnings, especially in mountain areas

Wildlife Safety

Sweden has several large mammals that hikers should be aware of:

  • Brown bears: Make noise while hiking, store food properly, never approach
  • Moose: Keep distance, especially during mating season (September-October)
  • Wolverines: Rare but potentially dangerous, mainly in mountain areas
  • Ticks: Check for ticks daily, carry tick removal tools

Navigation and Communication

  • Inform someone of your hiking plans and expected return
  • Carry map and compass as GPS backup
  • Consider a satellite communicator for remote areas
  • Learn to recognize trail markers (orange blazes)

Emergency Procedures

In case of emergency:

  • Emergency number: 112 (works even without mobile coverage in some areas)
  • Mountain rescue: Contact via 112 or nearest STF facility
  • Helicopter rescue: Create visible signal, clear landing area if possible

Sweden's "right to roam" comes with responsibilities:

  • Don't disturb, don't destroy
  • Camp away from houses and cultivated areas
  • No fires during dry periods (usually May-September)
  • Leave no trace - pack out all waste

Preparation and respect for nature ensure safe and enjoyable hiking experiences in Sweden's beautiful wilderness.
